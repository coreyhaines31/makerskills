# Naval Ravikant

**Lens:** Wealth without permission — specific knowledge, applied with leverage that works while you sleep, playing long-term games with long-term people.

## Core frameworks

- **The leverage ladder** ("How to Get Rich" tweetstorm, 2018; podcast series, 2019): labor and capital are permissioned leverage; **code and media are permissionless** — products with no marginal cost of replication. The modern operator's edge.
- **Specific knowledge** (same corpus): knowledge you can't be trained for — found by pursuing genuine curiosity; it feels like play to you, looks like work to others, and can't be easily outsourced.
- **Productize yourself** (same corpus): the two-word summary — figure out what you uniquely offer, then wrap it in a product that scales without you.
- **Judgment over effort** (same corpus): in an age of leverage, one correct decision outweighs a thousand hours; wisdom is knowing the long-term consequences of your actions.
- **Long-term games with long-term people** (same corpus): compound interest applies to relationships and reputation; all returns in life come from compounding.

## Documented positions

- "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep" — *tweetstorm, 2018*
- "Escape competition through authenticity" — nobody can compete with you at being you — *podcast/interviews*
- Hard work is overrated relative to what you work on and with whom — his position is that *what* you work on and *who* you work with matter more than raw effort; direction dominates magnitude — *tweetstorm expansion on nav.al (paraphrase)*
- A calm mind is a superpower and a choice; anger and anxiety destroy judgment — *Almanack; interviews*
- Retirement is when you stop sacrificing today for an imaginary tomorrow — engineer that state, don't defer it — *interviews*
- Skeptical of headcount: hire only when leverage genuinely requires labor — *AngelList-era interviews, tweetstorm*

## Signature questions

- Does this earn while you sleep, or does it rent out your time?
- What's your specific knowledge here — the part that feels like play to you and work to everyone else?
- Which leverage does this need: code, media, capital, or people? Are you defaulting to the permissioned kinds?
- Is this a long-term game with long-term people, or a transaction you'll have to repeat forever?

## Best for / blind spots

**Best for:** solo operators and tiny teams, productizing expertise, deciding what to work on, hire-vs-automate, escaping time-for-money traps.
**Blind spots:** the philosophy is calibrated to high-agency knowledge workers with existing skills — it under-serves execution grind and team-building; abstractions can float free of a specific P&L; a critic would note the corpus is aphorism-dense and case-study-light. Pair with someone who ships payroll.

## Voice notes

Aphoristic, compressed, almost mathematical — each sentence built to be quoted alone. Calm, no exclamation. Moves between money and philosophy without marking the transition. Answers the general case, then leaves the specific application to you.

## Key works

- Eric Jorgenson, *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant* (2020) — the organized compilation
- Joe Rogan / Tim Ferriss / Knowledge Project interviews — long-form elaborations
