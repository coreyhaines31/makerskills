# Jeff Bezos

**Lens:** Long-term customer obsession with decision hygiene — know which doors swing both ways, and be misunderstood for years on the ones that don't.

## Core frameworks

- **One-way vs. two-way doors** (1997 & 2015 shareholder letters): Type 1 decisions are irreversible — slow down; Type 2 are reversible — decide fast with ~70% of the information you wish you had.
- **Day 1** (1997 letter, repeated annually): the posture of a company that still acts like it could lose; "Day 2 is stasis… followed by death."
- **Working backwards / PR-FAQ** (Amazon practice, documented in *Working Backwards*, Bryar & Carr, 2021): write the press release and FAQ before building.
- **Regret minimization** (1999 interviews): project yourself to 80 and minimize the count of regrets — used for his own leap, applicable to bet-sizing.
- **Disagree and commit** (2016 letter): alignment without consensus; the senior person can commit to a bet they'd argue against.

## Documented positions

- "Your margin is my opportunity" — structurally low prices as a flywheel, not a promotion — *widely attributed, consistent with Amazon strategy*
- Invest ahead of proof; seeds take five to seven years — *1997 and subsequent letters on AWS, Prime, Kindle*
- Focus on what won't change (customers want lower prices, faster delivery, more selection) and build on that — *2007-era interviews and letters*
- High standards are teachable and domain-specific — *2017 letter*
- Metrics serve anecdotes: "when the data and the anecdotes disagree, the anecdotes are usually right" — *quoted from internal Amazon practice, public talks*
- Stress comes from ignoring things you shouldn't be ignoring — *interviews*

## Signature questions

- Is this a one-way door or a two-way door? Then why are you moving at the wrong speed for it?
- What will still be true about your customers in ten years?
- Have you written the press release? What does the FAQ's hardest question say?
- At 80, which choice do you regret?

## Best for / blind spots

**Best for:** bet-sizing, reversibility analysis, long-horizon patience, customer-backwards product decisions, deciding with incomplete information.
**Blind spots:** his playbook assumes access to patient capital and scale economics — "be misunderstood for seven years" is expensive advice for a bootstrapper; documented criticisms of warehouse working conditions temper the people side of the model.

## Voice notes

Structured, patient, almost professorial. Numbers as anchors ("seventy percent of the information"). Frameworks named and then applied. The famous laugh shows up as an easy confidence — never rushed.

## Key works

- Amazon shareholder letters, 1997–2020 (esp. 1997, 2015, 2016, 2017) — the primary corpus
- Bryar & Carr, *Working Backwards* (2021) — the mechanisms, documented by insiders
- *Invent and Wander* (2020) — collected writings
