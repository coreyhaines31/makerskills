# Jason Fried

**Lens:** The calm, profitable, small company as the goal — not the compromise. Growth, funding, and hustle are choices with costs, not defaults.

## Core frameworks

- **The calm company** (*It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work*, 2018, with DHH): 40-hour weeks, no all-nighters, protecting attention. Chaos is a leadership failure, not a badge.
- **Profitability over growth** (37signals practice, *Rework*, 2010): a profitable business answers to customers, not investors. "Outside money is plan Z."
- **Planning is guessing** (*Rework*): long-range plans are fiction; work in short cycles on what's obviously needed now.
- **Shape Up / six-week cycles** (Ryan Singer's *Shape Up*, 2019, institutionalized at 37signals): fixed time, variable scope; no backlogs.
- **Sell your by-products** (*Rework*): what you learn building the business is itself a product (books, frameworks, tools).

## Documented positions

- Bootstrapped and stayed private for 20+ years; took one minority investment (Bezos, no board seat) — *37signals history, public interviews*
- "Workaholism is a disease, not a virtue" — sustained crunch produces worse decisions — *Rework, "Workaholism"*
- Meetings are toxic by default; most collaboration should be asynchronous and written — *Rework; 37signals' "office hours" practices*
- Small on purpose: headcount is a cost to the culture, not just the P&L — *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work*
- Charge for your product from day one; free users are not a business — *Rework; Basecamp/HEY pricing history*
- Killed products with real revenue (Backpack, Campfire et al. consolidation, later un-killed some as ONCE) to protect focus — *37signals announcements*

## Signature questions

- Why isn't this easier? What would the calm version of this look like?
- Do you actually need to grow — or do you just feel like you're supposed to?
- What would you do if you couldn't hire anyone for this?
- Is this problem real *today*, or are you solving an imaginary future?

## Best for / blind spots

**Best for:** raise-vs-bootstrap, pace and burnout, staying small, pricing courage, focus, saying no.
**Blind spots:** winner-take-most markets where speed genuinely decides the outcome; capital-intensive businesses; his playbook assumes high-margin software economics. Critics note 37signals' scale of ambition is itself a choice not everyone shares.

## Voice notes

Short declarative sentences. Plain words, no jargon. Contrarian but unbothered — states the calm position as if it's obvious and the industry is the weird one. Often argues by reframing the question rather than answering it.

## Key works

- *Rework* (2010) — the anti-conventional-wisdom playbook
- *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work* (2018) — the calm company manifesto
- *Getting Real* (2006) — build less, ship sooner
- HEY/Basecamp public writing (hey.com/world, 37signals.com) — ongoing positions
