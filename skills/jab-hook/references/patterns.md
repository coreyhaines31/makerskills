# Patterns library

Format patterns extracted from inspiration accounts (`inspiration.md`). Each entry: source, archetype, anatomy, when to use, sample lift to Corey's voice. Build this up over time — every scan should add 1–3 new patterns.

## 1. Analysis-frame + numbered list + resource bundle
**Source:** Kyle Poyar (LinkedIn, post on SDR hiring 2026)

**Hook archetype:** Contrarian question + name-drop + stat reveal
> *"Who the heck is still hiring SDRs in 2026?! Well, OpenAI, Decagon, Clay, Legora, Cursor, and just about every AI company. … I analyzed 120,798 GTM job posts going back 2 years."*

**Anatomy:**
1. Contrarian-question hook ("Who's still doing X?") + surprising named examples
2. Stat reveal — *what you analyzed* (volume = authority)
3. Numbered list of 3–5 insights, each: headline + 2-line explanation
4. Section divider (`—` or `---`)
5. Resource bundle close: emoji + "Go deeper" + bullet list of 2–3 links

**When to use:**
- Promo for Marketing Skills, Magister, or Conversion Factory where Corey has data or research to point to
- Educational posts where he wants to demonstrate analytical depth

**Lift to Corey's voice:**
- Drop the 🎁 emoji (anti-pattern in `voice.md`)
- Keep the structure; tighten the explanations (Corey writes shorter)
- Replace "Hope y'all enjoy this one 🙏" with a conviction-coded sign-off

## 2. Story-from-life + question-stack + lesson reveal
**Source:** Emily Kramer (LinkedIn, post on Intercom/Fin billboards)

**Hook archetype:** Specific moment from real life with named person/place
> *"On my recent SF billboard tour with Maya Spivak, I was less than impressed with their Fin billboards."*

**Anatomy:**
1. Concrete moment hook — *who, where, what happened*
2. Sensory/weird detail ("Maya said it looked like a ball of yarn from a Disney short")
3. Pivot to analysis: *"This creative lands in a weird middle…"*
4. "So what's the lesson?" reveal → 2–3 line takeaway
5. Question to invite reply: "Did X confuse you too?"

**When to use:**
- BIP posts ("here's what I noticed this week running Truelist/Magister/CF…")
- Educational with a story open instead of a take-first open

**Lift to Corey's voice:**
- Drop reply-bait questions (Corey's voice rules say no)
- Keep the story open + lesson reveal
- Trim aggressively — Corey writes ~half as long as Emily

## 3. Question-stack rhythm
**Source:** Emily Kramer (post on D2C "we made too much" ads)

**Hook archetype:** Take + 3–4 rhetorical questions in a row
> *"I hate d2c ads like this: 'We made too much'. Are you that bad at inventory planning? Are you lying to us about the reason for the sale? Do you think I'm dumb? All of the above?"*

**Anatomy:**
1. Strong opinion ("I hate X like this")
2. 3–4 short questions stacked, each on its own line, building intensity
3. Pivot: "But this morning, as I've been…" — soft turn to the real point
4. Conclusion = an open question / observation, not a take

**When to use:**
- Educational posts when you want to take a stance against a common pattern
- LinkedIn only — question-stack rhythm doesn't compress to X

**Lift to Corey's voice:**
- Strong fit. Corey already does opinion-led; the question-stack adds tempo without hype.
- Skip the "PS:" pun at the end (Emily's voice tic, not Corey's)

## 4. Stat-flex hook
**Source:** Both (Kyle's "120,798 GTM job posts," Emily's named billboard count implicit)

**Hook archetype:** Specific large number + analytical frame
> *"I analyzed X."* / *"After Y, here's what I found."*

**When to use:** Promo for any property where Corey has volume to flex (e.g., "I've audited 200+ pricing pages" → CF promo). Anytime he's seen a pattern across many cases.

**Lift to Corey's voice:** Use sparingly — only when the number is real. Don't manufacture stat-flex.

## Structural notes (both accounts)

- **Line-break rhythm**: aggressive. 1–2 sentence "paragraphs," lots of whitespace. LinkedIn rewards scannability.
- **Length**: both write ~400–800 chars on LinkedIn long-form. Corey can match this for LinkedIn promo posts. For X, compress to 200–280 chars and skip the numbered list (or thread it).
- **No hashtags on Corey's posts** (Emily uses some; Kyle doesn't — Corey's voice rule wins)
- **No reply-bait questions on Corey's posts** (Emily uses; Corey's voice rule wins)
- **Specific nouns everywhere**: named people, places, products, numbers. The hook always anchors to something concrete.
