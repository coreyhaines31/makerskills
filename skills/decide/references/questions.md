# The 38 questions

From [The 37signals Guide to Making Decisions](https://37signals.com/how-we-make-decisions). Premise: *"A company is essentially two things: a group of people and a collection of decisions."*

Categorized for triage. The `decide` skill picks 6–8 per decision based on the situation — never all 38.

---

## Need-to-decide (do we even decide?)

- **Q1** — Why are we deciding anything at all? Does a decision actually need to be made here?
- **Q4** — Why hasn't this decision been made already? Why didn't we decide before?
- **Q5** — What's taking so long to make this decision? Why are we hesitating? What does that reveal?
- **Q10** — What would happen if we just didn't make the decision?
- **Q22** — When do we have to decide?
- **Q31** — Do you even care which way this goes? If not, why are you involved?

## Who decides

- **Q2** — Is the right person making this decision? Not the right role, but the right person with the right information, context, and insight? Who's merely chiming in?
- **Q21** — Could this decision be a good one for someone else to practice making?
- **Q27** — Would another opinion help or hinder?
- **Q35** — Are we asking multiple people to make a decision that one person should be making?

## Reversibility & size

- **Q7** — Can we make this decision smaller? Can we take one big decision and turn it into three smaller ones?
- **Q8** — How easily can we reverse the decision?
- **Q14** — Is there even a wrong decision?
- **Q23** — Will this be a one-and-done decision, or will this be a repeating decision?

## First instinct

- **Q9** — What was our first instinct on this decision? Are we now just walking around in circles trying to justify that gut reaction with data?
- **Q28** — If we were forced to make a decision right this second, what would it be?

## Cost & payoff

- **Q13** — How can we make this decision easier? What parts can we eliminate from consideration?
- **Q20** — Will this decision make more work for people that don't have extra time for that work? Or will it eliminate work?
- **Q36** — Is the return on effort worth it?
- **Q37** — What gets easier if we make this decision? What gets harder? Will easier remain easier in the long term, or is it short-term easy but long-term hard? And vice versa.
- **Q38** — In the end, is this about money?

## Context & impact

- **Q6** — Why would someone else make a different decision? What's the other side — or two or three — look like?
- **Q17** — What other decisions will be impacted by this decision?
- **Q18** — Will this decision eliminate the need to make other decisions, or will it create the necessity to make even more decisions?
- **Q24** — Is anyone outside the company depending on this, or is this a decision of our own making?
- **Q25** — How does this decision impact customers vs. impact us?
- **Q29** — Where do we think we'd be today if we made this decision 90 days ago?
- **Q30** — Is there anything in this decision we'd regret if we didn't take X, Y, or Z into consideration?

## Signal & confidence

- **Q11** — What happened the last time we made a decision like this?
- **Q19** — What missing information would lead to making a different decision?
- **Q26** — Is this primarily a data-based decision, or an intuition, gut-based decision?
- **Q32** — When and how will we know whether the decision was the right one, or if it even mattered?
- **Q33** — When the consequences of our decision appear, are they likely to be visible with the naked eye or do they require a microscope to detect? If the latter, does it even matter?

## Principles

- **Q34** — What principles are we bending if we make this decision?

## Time test

- **Q3** — If we remove the immediate impact, how do we think we'll feel about this decision a year from now?
- **Q15** — Do we anticipate making a different decision if we wait until tomorrow morning to make it?
- **Q16** — Is any decision better than no decision, or is no decision better than any decision?

## Emotional check

- **Q12** — What are we looking forward to after the decision is made? What are we afraid of?

## House additions (not from the 37signals set)

Questions the set was missing in practice. Kept separate so the 38 above stay faithful to the source.

- **Q39** — Opportunity cost: if we say yes to this, what are we saying no to? What does this displace — time, money, focus — and is what it displaces worth less than this? *(Added after a reader pointed out the original 38 never ask it directly — the nearest are Q36's return-on-effort and Q37's easier/harder.)*

## Triage cheat-sheet (default selections)

**Always:** Q1, Q2, Q8, Q9, Q3

**Add for irreversible:** Q14, Q17, Q34
**Add for time-pressure:** Q5, Q15, Q22
**Add for multi-person:** Q21, Q27, Q35
**Add for analytical:** Q19, Q26
**Add for recurring:** Q11, Q23
**Add for customer-facing:** Q24, Q25
**Add for money:** Q36, Q38, Q39
**Add for big time/focus commitments:** Q39, Q36, Q20
**Add for "I'm stuck":** Q4, Q10, Q31
